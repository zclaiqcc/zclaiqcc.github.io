About

Who

Bullet Journal® (or BuJo® for short) was created by Ryder Carroll, an digital product designer and author living in Brooklyn, NY. Diagnosed with learning disabilities early in life, he was forced to figure out alternate ways to be focused and productive. Through years of trial and error, he developed a methodology that went far beyond simple organization. Now he focuses on helping others learn what the Bullet Journal method is truly about: the art of intentional living.

Bullet Journal®（缩写为 BuJo®）是由居住在纽约州布鲁克林的数字产品设计师和作家 Ryder Carroll 创建的。他很早就被诊断出有学习障碍，他不得不想出其他的方法来集中精力和提高工作效率。通过多年的尝试和错误，他开发出了一种非常非常简单的组织方法。现在，他专注于帮助他人学习 Bullet Journal 方法的真正意义：有意生活的艺术。

What

Though it does require a journal, Bullet Journal® is a methodology. It’s best described as a mindfulness practice disguised as a productivity system. It’s designed to help you organize your what while you remain mindful of your why. The goal of the Bullet Journal is to help its practitioners (Bullet Journalists) live intentional lives, ones that are both productive and meaningful.

虽然它确实需要一本日记本，但 Bullet Journal® 是一种方法论。它最适合被描述为一种类似于生产力系统的思维练习。它的目的是帮助你在组织你的“是什么”的同时，保持对“为什么”的关注。Bullet Journal 的目标是帮助它的实践者（Bullet Journalists）过上有意义的生活，既富有成效，又有意义。

Learn

Rapid Logging

Be it for taking notes or journaling, studies keep identifying benefits of writing by hand. That said, it takes time and can be unorganized. How can we enjoy the benefits while avoiding the shortcomings of hand writing? Rapid Logging.

Rapid Logging is the language in which the Bullet Journal is written. In short, it’s a way of capturing information as bulleted lists. Let’s start with the basics.

不管是做笔记还是写日记，越来越多的研究不断地表明手写的好处。但是有一个缺点是手写需要时间，而且可能会有条理不清的问题。如何才能在享受手写带来的好处的同时，避免手写的缺点呢？快速记录法（Rapid Logging）。

所谓快速记录，就是指 Bullet Journal 的写作语言。简而言之，就是把信息记录成带标记的列表的方式。让我们从基础知识入手。

Bullet

If Rapid Logging is language the BuJo is written in, Bullets are the syntax. Bullets are short-form sentences paired with symbols that visually categorize your entries into: Tasks, Events, or Notes. Let’s break it down…

如果 Rapid Logging 是 BuJo 所使用的语言，那么 Bullets 就是语法。Bullets 是简短的句子与符号的配对，可以直观地将您的条目分类为: 任务，事件，或笔记。让我们来分解一下…

• Tasks 任务：要做的事
- Notes 笔记：不想忘记的事
o Events 事件：值得及时记录的事

Tasks

Tasks are represented by a simple dot •. We use a dot instead of a checkbox because it’s fast, clean, and can easily be transformed to reflect the state of the Task. Tasks can have one of five states:

任务用一个简单的点 • 来表示。我们使用圆点而不是复选框，因为它快速、干净，并且可以很容易地转换来反映任务的状态。任务可以有五种状态：

• 任务未完成
x 任务已完成
> 任务迁移到集合
< 任务迁移到未来的日志
• 任务无关紧要

Events

Events are represented by the open circle o Bullet. Events are date-related entries that can either be scheduled (e.g. “Charlie’s birthday”) or logged after they occur (e.g. “signed the lease”).

Our experiences can be complicated and distracting. Rather than trying to capture the way you feel in the moment, keep your Event entries short and objective. It will increase the odds of us writing them down. The important thing is to have a record of your experience so that you can learn from it.

事件是由开放的圆圈 o 表示。事件是与日期相关的条目，既可以是计划的（如 “查理的生日”），也可以在事件发生后记录下来（如 “签署租约”）。

我们的经历可能会很复杂，也会让人分心。与其试图捕捉到你当时的感受，不如让你的 Event 条目简短而客观。这样会增加我们写下来的几率。最重要的是要记录下你的经历，这样你就可以从中学习。

o Tom 的离职日
o 和 LK 一起看《神奇女侠》
o 为了清空头脑走路回家

Notes

Notes are represented with a dash –. Notes include: facts, ideas, thoughts, and observations. They’re used to capture information or data you don’t want to forget. This Bullet works well for meeting, lecture, or classroom notes.

注释用破折号 – 表示。笔记包括：事实、观点、想法、思想和观察。它们是用来捕捉你不想忘记的信息或数据。这个 Bullets 很适合用于会议、演讲或课堂笔记。

– 03.25：办公室关门维修
– Jack 有麸质不耐症
– 用 mayo 代替黄油

Mix and Match

Tasks, Events, and Notes will help you quickly capture your thoughts as they bubble up throughout the day. Don’t worry about logging them in any particular order. The important thing is to get them out of your head, and onto the page.

任务、事件和笔记将帮助您快速记录您一天中涌现的想法。不要担心按照任何特定的顺序记录它们。最重要的是把它们从你的脑海中抽离出来，放到页面上。

Nesting

Nesting Bullets can add some much needed color to your entries. For example, nest Notes under an Event to capture important details. Nest subtasks under the main Task to break things down into a series of steps.

嵌套 Bullets 可以为你的条目添加一些非常需要的颜色。例如，在 Event 下嵌套 Notes，以捕捉重要的细节。在主任务下嵌套子任务，将事情分解成一系列步骤。

o 和 Leigh 共进晚餐
　- @El Salvadors
　- 讨论如何帮助 Jane
•Claire: 安排生日蛋糕
　•订蛋糕
　•拿蛋糕
　-需要无麸质

Signifiers

Signifiers are symbols that give your entries additional context at a glance. They’re placed to the left of Bullets so they stick out, making them easy to spot when scanning your pages. Here are two useful examples, but feel free to come up with your own.

* = Priority: Used to mark the most important things on your list. Use it sparingly. If everything is a priority, nothing is.

! = Inspiration: Great ideas, personal mantras, and genius insights will never be misplaced again!

Signifiers 是一种符号，可以让您的条目只需要瞥一眼就能迅速了解额外的上下文信息。它们被放置在 Bullets 的左边，这样它们就很容易在扫描页面时被注意到。这里有两个有用的例子，但你也可以自己想出你自己的例子。

* = 优先级（备注：这里是星号※，字体很小看不太清楚）。用来标记你清单上最重要的事情。尽量少用。如果所有的事情都是优先级，那么没有什么是优先级。

! = 灵感。伟大的想法，个人的口头禅，和天才的洞察力，永远不会再放错地方了!

　　•Claire: 安排生日蛋糕
　　　* •订蛋糕
　　　　　•拿蛋糕
　　　　　-需要无麸质
* •Niclas: 打给旅行社
! -《碎纸机之歌》
